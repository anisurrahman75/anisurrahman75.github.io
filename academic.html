I am a Senior Software Engineer at AppsCode, where I build the distributed systems that keep managed databases alive — backup and recovery, point-in-time restore, and multi-cloud storage running at 99.99% uptime.

My work sits at the intersection of distributed systems, cloud-native infrastructure, and storage. Over the past three years I have designed sharded Kubernetes operators coordinating over a thousand database instances, engineered WAL-based point-in-time recovery, and built migration pipelines that move multi-terabyte datasets in hours. These systems left me with concrete research questions about consistency under failure, the true cost of recovery, and how storage should behave when the network and hardware do not cooperate.

I am applying for a fully-funded PhD / MS in the United States, where I hope to turn production-scale systems experience into rigorous, publishable systems research.
